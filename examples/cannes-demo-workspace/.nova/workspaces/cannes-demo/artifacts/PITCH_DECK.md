# Pitch Deck: The Kill List

## Logline

A disgraced intelligence analyst finds her own name on a classified assassination ledger—and the only person who can help her is the agent sent to kill her.

---

## Visual Thesis

> *"Paranoia as architecture."*

The Kill List unfolds in a world of concrete, neon, and rain. Our visual language borrows from brutalist Berlin infrastructure and the cold intimacy of Nordic noir. Every frame should feel like a surveillance photograph: composed, tense, and slightly too close.

### Key Visual References
- Berlin’s Stasi Museum (control rooms, filing cabinets, fluorescent dread)
- The nocturnal streets of Kreuzberg (neon, wet asphalt, isolation)
- The safe-house as pressure cooker (single location, shifting power dynamics)

## Casting Direction

### Elena Voss (Lead)
- **Profile:** Late 30s. Fierce intelligence masked by exhaustion. Not an action hero—she thinks her way out.
- **Wish List:** Sibel Kekilli, Antje Traue, or an emerging Eastern European lead.

### The Handler (Supporting Lead)
- **Profile:** Early 40s. Professional, world-weary, unexpectedly empathetic. The audience should root for him even as he holds a gun.
- **Wish List:** Daniel Brühl, Nikolaj Coster-Waldau, or equivalent European draw.
